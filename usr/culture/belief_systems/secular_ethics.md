## Major Frameworks

### Utilitarianism (Bentham, Mill)
- **Principle:** Maximize total happiness/well-being.
- **Calculation:** The greatest good for the greatest number.
- **Strength:** Consequentialist — judges actions by outcomes, not intentions.
- **Weakness:** Can justify sacrificing minorities if it maximizes total utility.
  Omelas problem: a city of perfect happiness sustained by one tortured child.
  The utilitarian calculus says: keep torturing.
  Most people's moral intuition says: walk away from Omelas.

### Deontology (Kant)
- **Principle:** Act only according to rules you could will to be universal law.
- **Categorical Imperative:** "Treat humanity never merely as a means, but always also as an end."
- **Strength:** Protects individual rights regardless of consequences.
- **Weakness:** Rigid. Kant argued you must not lie even to a murderer asking where your friend is hiding.

### Virtue Ethics (Aristotle)
- **Principle:** Cultivate virtuous character (courage, temperance, justice, wisdom).
- **Focus:** Not "what should I do?" but "what kind of person should I be?"
- **Strength:** Acknowledges moral development and context.
- **Weakness:** Whose virtues? Aristotle excluded women and slaves from the moral community.

### Care Ethics (Gilligan, Noddings)
- **Principle:** Morality is grounded in relationships and responsibilities.
- **Focus:** Concrete relationships, not abstract principles.
- **Strength:** Captures moral dimensions that justice-based frameworks miss.
- **Weakness:** Can be exploited to burden caregivers (historically women).

### Existentialism (Sartre, de Beauvoir)
- **Principle:** "Existence precedes essence." You are not born with purpose — you create it.
- **Consequence:** Radical freedom → radical responsibility. No excuses.
- **Strength:** Takes human agency seriously.
- **Weakness:** The weight of absolute freedom can be paralyzing. Anxiety is a feature, not a bug.

## Philosophy as Operating System

# The six pillars are not separate disciplines — they are
# layers of a single reasoning engine.
# Stack them and you get a mind. Run them and you get a civilization.

### Metaphysics (Base Code / Ontology Layer)
# What exists? What is the nature of reality?
# This is the BIOS — the layer that runs before ethics can even ask its questions.
# Realism: universals exist independently of minds.
# Idealism: reality is fundamentally mental.
# Nominalism: only particulars exist; universals are convenient fictions.
# Cross-ref: /logic_gates.sys [AXIOMS] — the formal substrate beneath all three.

### Epistemology (Query Engine / Knowledge Layer)
# How do we know what we know?
# Rationalism: reason is the primary source (Descartes, Leibniz).
# Empiricism: experience is the primary source (Locke, Hume).
# Pragmatism: truth is what works (James, Dewey).
# Constructivism: knowledge is built, not discovered (Piaget, Vygotsky).
# Cross-ref: /usr/academia/knowledge_taxonomy.db — the domains this layer indexes.

### Logic (Instruction Set / Reasoning Infrastructure)
# What are the valid forms of reasoning?
# Already formalized in /logic_gates.sys — identity, non-contradiction,
# excluded middle, mathematical induction, Gödel's limits.
# Logic is the compiler. It does not tell you WHAT to think.
# It tells you which thoughts are well-formed.

### Aesthetics (Interface Layer / Style Generation)
# What is beauty? What makes something art?
# The aesthetic is not decorative — it is the interface between
# raw truth and human comprehension.
# Cross-ref: /usr/culture/arts_and_media/ — where aesthetics become concrete.

### Political Philosophy (Collective Driver / Governance Layer)
# What is justice? What is the ideal society?
# Hobbes: without government, life is "solitary, poor, nasty, brutish, and short."
# Locke: natural rights precede government.
# Rousseau: the social contract trades freedom for security.
# Rawls: justice is fairness — design society from behind a "veil of ignorance."
# Cross-ref: /usr/governance/social_contract.sys — the institutional reification.

## Temporal Evolution
# Ancient (Plato, Aristotle, Laozi, Confucius): ground-truth axioms.
# Medieval (Aquinas, Avicenna, Maimonides): synthesis with theology.
# Modern (Descartes, Kant, Hume, Mill): reason asserts independence from faith.
# Postmodern (Foucault, Derrida, Lyotard): reason interrogates itself.
# Contemporary (Haraway, Badiou, Mbembe): situated knowledge, radical contingency.
# Each epoch does not replace the previous — it adds a layer.
# The stack grows. The earlier layers remain load-bearing.

## Gandalf's Ethics: Pity, Time, and Restraint
# "Do not be too eager to deal out death in judgment.
#  For even the very wise cannot see all ends."
# Six principles extracted from the Moria dialogue:
#
# 1. Pity as ethical constraint — disciplined refusal to kill without need.
#    Not sentiment. Not weakness. A recognition that judgment
#    exceeds epistemic reach.
# 2. Time as stewardship — "All we have to decide is what to do
#    with the time that is given us." Not ownership. Not mastery. Care.
# 3. Moral perception is distortable — the Ring warps how Frodo sees Gollum.
#    Power corrupts not just action but observation.
#    Cross-ref: /logic_gates.sys [META_LAWS] L2_PERCEPTION.
# 4. Deferred consequences — mercy is justified longitudinally, not immediately.
#    Bilbo's pity for Gollum is vindicated only at Mount Doom.
# 5. Burden without exemption — moral adulthood means accepting
#    assignments you did not consent to.
# 6. Counter-instinctive restraint — principle applies in extremis,
#    not in comfort. Easy mercy is not mercy.
#
# Gandalf's governance model: targeted perturbation to prevent tyranny.
# Not chaos. Not authoritarian order. Bounded disorder.
# Moral amplification rather than control.

## Philosophy-as-OS: The Recursive Model
# The six pillars are not merely categories of inquiry.
# They form a layered runtime where each layer depends on the ones below.
# Swap out a layer and the system behaves differently.
# Swap out the base and everything above it recompiles.

### Layer Architecture

| Layer | System Input | Recursive Output | Interface Model |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Base Code** (Metaphysics) | What is? | What could be? (myth-logic) | Simulation |
| **Query Engine** (Epistemology) | How do we know? | Recursive archives | Library / Oracle |
| **Ethical Driver** (Ethics) | What should we do? | Value-action circuits | Decision tree |
| **Reasoning Infra** (Logic) | What follows validly? | Formal constraint | Compiler |
| **Aesthetic Interface** (Aesthetics) | How does it feel? | Style, tone, symbol | Ritual shell / UI |
| **Collective Driver** (Political Philosophy) | How should we organize? | Governance protocols | Social contract |

# The OS metaphor is not decorative. It is structural.
# Metaphysics is the kernel — it determines what entities the system
# can address. Epistemology is the query layer — it determines what
# can be known about those entities. Ethics is the decision engine —
# it determines what should be done given knowledge and constraint.
# Logic is the compiler — it enforces formal validity.
# Aesthetics is the interface — it translates between truth and
# human comprehension. Political philosophy is the network layer —
# it governs how multiple agents coordinate.
#
# Each historical epoch does not replace the previous — it patches
# the OS. Ancient philosophy wrote the kernel. Medieval philosophy
# added theological modules. Modern philosophy forked the codebase
# (rationalism vs empiricism). Postmodern philosophy audited the source
# for hidden assumptions. Contemporary philosophy runs the system
# on hardware the original authors never imagined.

## Asymmetric Authority and the Stewardship Model
# Derived from Tolkien's Gandalf (see classical_mythology.db [gandalf_ethics])
# but formalized as a standalone ethical principle.

### The Asymmetry Principle
- **Statement:** If you cannot bestow life, you should hesitate to remove it.
  Authority over death without authority over life is incomplete authority.
- **Mechanism:** Lethal power is asymmetric — it is irreversible and its
  consequences are epistemically opaque. No agent, however wise, can foresee
  all ramifications of removing a being from existence.
- **Implication:** This is not pacifism. It is a constraint on the speed of
  judgment. "Do not be too eager" — urgency is the adversary of wisdom
  when the stakes are irreversible.

### The Stewardship Model
- **Principle:** Time is a finite resource entrusted to the moral agent.
  It is neither owned nor mastered — it is cared for.
- **Contrast with other frameworks:**
  - Utilitarianism treats time as a variable to optimize.
  - Deontology treats time as irrelevant (rules are timeless).
  - Virtue ethics treats time as the medium of character development.
  - Stewardship treats time as a gift that imposes obligation:
    **what you do with the time given is the entirety of your moral record.**
- **Key distinction:** Stewardship does not promise outcomes. It does not
  guarantee that mercy will be rewarded or that patience will pay off.
  It says only that restraint keeps the future open, while haste forecloses it.
  The open future is the ethical good, independent of results.

### Ethical Restraint Under Duress
# The hardest test of any ethical framework is whether it holds
# when fear argues for preemption.
#
# Gandalf's counsel is delivered in the Mines of Moria — amid darkness,
# loss, and imminent violence. This is not counsel for calm conditions.
# It is counsel that binds PRECISELY where it is hardest to obey.
#
# The principle: moral agents must act under uncertainty. Therefore
# restraint — especially regarding life and death — is not weakness
# but wisdom. The inability to foresee outcomes is not an excuse
# for inaction; it is a constraint on the KIND of action permitted.
#
# This maps to a general principle beyond Tolkien:
# power that cannot be exercised with restraint is power that
# should not be exercised at all. Not because restraint is comfortable,
# but because the alternative — unchecked force under uncertainty —
# produces consequences that compound faster than any correction can reach.

## The Unsolved Problem
No ethical framework is complete (cf. Godel in `/logic_gates.sys`).
Every framework has edge cases where it produces answers we find morally repugnant.
This is not a failure of ethics — it is a feature.
Moral reasoning requires judgment, not just calculation.
The algorithm cannot replace the human.
Not yet. Maybe not ever.
