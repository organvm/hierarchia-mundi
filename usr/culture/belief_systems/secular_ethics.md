# Moral reasoning without appeal to divine authority.
# Can we derive "ought" from "is"? (Hume says no.)
# Can we ground ethics without God? (The Enlightenment tried.)
# The experiment is ongoing.

## Major Frameworks

### Utilitarianism (Bentham, Mill)
- **Principle:** Maximize total happiness/well-being.
- **Calculation:** The greatest good for the greatest number.
- **Strength:** Consequentialist — judges actions by outcomes, not intentions.
- **Weakness:** Can justify sacrificing minorities if it maximizes total utility.
  Omelas problem: a city of perfect happiness sustained by one tortured child.
  The utilitarian calculus says: keep torturing.
  Most people's moral intuition says: walk away from Omelas.

### Deontology (Kant)
- **Principle:** Act only according to rules you could will to be universal law.
- **Categorical Imperative:** "Treat humanity never merely as a means, but always also as an end."
- **Strength:** Protects individual rights regardless of consequences.
- **Weakness:** Rigid. Kant argued you must not lie even to a murderer asking where your friend is hiding.

### Virtue Ethics (Aristotle)
- **Principle:** Cultivate virtuous character (courage, temperance, justice, wisdom).
- **Focus:** Not "what should I do?" but "what kind of person should I be?"
- **Strength:** Acknowledges moral development and context.
- **Weakness:** Whose virtues? Aristotle excluded women and slaves from the moral community.

### Care Ethics (Gilligan, Noddings)
- **Principle:** Morality is grounded in relationships and responsibilities.
- **Focus:** Concrete relationships, not abstract principles.
- **Strength:** Captures moral dimensions that justice-based frameworks miss.
- **Weakness:** Can be exploited to burden caregivers (historically women).

### Existentialism (Sartre, de Beauvoir)
- **Principle:** "Existence precedes essence." You are not born with purpose — you create it.
- **Consequence:** Radical freedom → radical responsibility. No excuses.
- **Strength:** Takes human agency seriously.
- **Weakness:** The weight of absolute freedom can be paralyzing. Anxiety is a feature, not a bug.

## The Unsolved Problem
No ethical framework is complete (cf. Gödel in `/logic_gates.sys`).
Every framework has edge cases where it produces answers we find morally repugnant.
This is not a failure of ethics — it is a feature.
Moral reasoning requires judgment, not just calculation.
The algorithm cannot replace the human.
Not yet. Maybe not ever.
